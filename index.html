About

Signals, cognition, and computational models.

I am a neuroscience researcher with a background in psychology, neuroscience, EEG analysis, and machine learning. My work focuses on how neural and physiological signals can be used to study cognitive states, emotion regulation, fatigue, cognitive load, and human–technology interaction.

Even if my primary research is in this area, I'm often curious about other applications of signal processing — for instance, I also have experience in AI for medical care, working on laryngeal motility assessment and obstructive sleep apnoea detection.

Research interests

What I work on

01

EEG markers of cognition

Neural activity related to cognitive load, fatigue, attention, emotion regulation, and ongoing brain dynamics.

02

Machine learning for neural data

Classification, prediction, and interpretation pipelines for EEG, physiological, behavioural, and multimodal signals.

03

Digital phenotyping

Modelling wellbeing, workload, and adaptive human–computer interaction through wrist-worn sensors (PPG, EDA) and other digital and physiological markers.
